# Report paragraph: completion-scenario diagnostics

A separate diagnostic was performed for the scenario-frequency slots that were not completed in the raw comparison set but were recovered by the c0 completion runs. Within the cleaned comparison set, the relevant completion cases were s01 at 80 Hz and s03/s19 at 40 Hz. Both c0x09 and c0x11 recovered these slots with successful OCP permutation sets, indicating that the raw missing cases should not be interpreted as direct physical infeasibility. Instead, they are better understood as boundary cases where the hardware-search/OCP pipeline is sensitive to the initial hardware envelope and solver trajectory.

The scenario-feature comparison suggests that these completion scenarios are somewhat more demanding in deck-motion-related quantities, especially deck heave amplitude, peak-to-peak heave, and peak deck vertical speed. However, the completion group contains only three scenarios, so this observation should be treated as diagnostic rather than statistically conclusive. The hardware-burden comparison also shows that the completion scenarios are slightly more expensive on average, but they do not form a completely separate class. This supports a careful interpretation: the primary ranking should remain based on a cleaned common-success set, while the completion runs demonstrate that some raw missing cases lie close to a solver/search-sensitive feasibility boundary.
